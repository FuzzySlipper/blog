## CRITICAL NEW DEVELOPMENT NOT IN CONTEXT BLURB (APR 9)

**Trump announced a US Navy blockade of Iranian ports on April 12–13 after the Islamabad talks collapsed.**

Multiple sources confirm:
- CNBC (April 13): "Oil prices near $100 as U.S. Navy blockades Iran's ports after peace talks fail"
- The National (April 13): "Oil surges as US-Iran talks fail and Trump announces Strait of Hormuz blockade"
- Al Jazeera (April 13): "Oil prices surge past $103 a barrel after US announces blockade of Iran"

This is a double-blockade situation: Iran's permission-based Hormuz transit regime (pre-existing, ~91% closed to normal traffic) + US Navy blockade of Iranian ports. Different in kind from the prior context: Iran was *charging tolls* for transit; the US blockade is a maritime enforcement action against Iranian ports directly.

The April 14 partial reversal (Brent $103 → $94.79) came on Vance saying a deal is "still possible" — not on the blockade being lifted. The blockade status as of April 15 is unclear; the "still possible" language is the Trump admin's characteristic ambiguity.

**Confidence: Moderate-High** (multiple independent outlets confirming; CNBC and The National both have financial market readership with incentive to be accurate on market-moving facts).

**IEA April 14 Oil Market Report (primary source — institutional, high confidence):**
- Global oil supply fell **10.1 mb/d** to 97 mb/d in March — the **largest supply disruption in recorded history**
- Hormuz transit: 20 mb/d (February) → **3.8 mb/d** (early April)
- Global oil demand contraction: **800 kb/d YoY in March**; **2.3 mb/d in April** (worst since COVID)
- Full-year 2026 demand now forecast to **contract 80 kb/d** (prior estimate: growth)

**Bank forecasts (secondary reporting on research notes — moderate confidence):**
- Goldman Sachs: Q2 $90; $100+ if Hormuz shut another month; extreme scenario $135+
- JPMorgan: Q2 $100 avg / Q3 $90 / Q4 $80; **$150 if blockade extends to mid-May** — explicitly called "almost certainly a global economic contraction"

- ANZ: Above $90 remainder of year

**What the spike tells us:** The ~17% fall on April 8 confirmed markets had been pricing in a Hormuz reopening scenario as the most likely path. The collapse reversed roughly half of that move. Markets were not simply "pricing in extended conflict" before Islamabad — they were partially pricing in a deal. That assumption is now gone.

## SAUDI ARABIA — FISCAL BIND

The most financially stressed large GCC state.

**Fiscal arithmetic:**
- SAMA foreign reserves: $434B (February 2026), down from $451B in January — **$17B monthly drawdown** before the worst war-period pressure hit
- Goldman Sachs fiscal deficit estimate: **6.6% of GDP** (~$73B annually), double the official 3.3% forecast
- IMF fiscal break-even: $86.60/bbl (central government); Bloomberg Economics PIF-inclusive: **$108–111/bbl**
- Current Brent: ~$95. Saudi Red Sea exports post-pipeline restoration (April 12): ~2.47 mb/d — materially below pre-war Hormuz volumes
- At $95 Brent and constrained export volumes: Saudi running approximately **$30–45M/day deficit** vs. the PIF-inclusive break-even

**PIF structural data:**
- AUM: ~$941B (2024)
- Cash reserves: **~$15B** (late 2024) — lowest since 2020
- Annual Aramco dividend income lost due to production cuts: **~$6B**
- Construction contracts awarded: $71B (2024) → **below $30B (2025)** — 60% collapse
- PIF's share of those awards: **38% → 14%**
- Giga-project write-downs: **$8B**
- April 7, 2026: PIF signed MoU with King Street Capital for private credit in MENA — **seeking external capital for domestic projects rather than deploying own balance sheet**. This is a structural signal.

**The paradox (April 15):** Saudi Arabia is simultaneously:
1. Running SAMA reserves down $17B/month
2. Raising **$7B in a three-tranche dollar bond deal on April 15**
3. Having its SWF seek external private credit

This is not Treasury-selling-panic behavior — it is a sovereign borrowing to fund a fiscal gap while depleting reserves. The $300B BofA Treasury-selling scenario and the "Saudi is selling Treasuries" narrative need to be distinguished from this: Saudi is tapping debt markets to fund the gap, not liquidating assets in panic. Confirmed Saudi TIC data shows $14.7B Treasury reduction in **January** — pre-war. War-period Treasury data releases April 30.

## QATAR — QCB PREEMPTIVE PACKAGE

**Significant action: QCB activated banking support on April 13** — the morning after the Islamabad collapse:
- **Unlimited QAR repo facility** against eligible securities
- **Term repo facility** up to 3 months
- **Reserve requirement cut: 4.5% → 3.5%** (freeing ~QAR 8–10B in liquidity)
- **Loan deferrals**: Banks may offer up to 3-month principal+interest postponement

Official framing: "precautionary." S&P data: Qatar's liquid assets at **181% of GDP**.

QCB Director's framing is boilerplate resilience language. The timing — the morning after the collapse — is not. A reserve requirement cut is a real monetary policy action, not a press release.

**The $6B frozen Iranian assets:**
- Iran claimed at the Islamabad table that the US agreed to unfreeze the $6B held in Qatar
- April 11 (same day): US senior official told CBS: "Not True"
- QCB confirmed April 11: Funds still frozen, require US Treasury approval for release
- Post-collapse: No movement. Remains a potential bargaining chip for a hypothetical Round 2

**QatarEnergy / Ras Laffan:**
- Long-term force majeure declared on contracts with China, South Korea, Italy, Belgium buyers
- Annual lost LNG revenue: **~$20B** (12.8 mtpa / ~17% of exports)
- QELNG North 1: Two of three trains restarted after April 7 ceasefire announcement; a third may follow soon
- North site (undamaged): Could partially restart within ~1 month of a real ceasefire
- South site (direct strike target): **End of summer 2026** at earliest
- Specialty equipment (3 global manufacturers only): **2–4 year delivery wait**
- New diversification signal: QatarEnergy announced a **hydrocarbon discovery off the Congo coast** on April 13 — geographically hedging against Gulf infrastructure vulnerability in real time

**The Abu Dhabi vs. Dubai divergence remains structurally accurate:**

- Dubai (real estate bonds, aviation, data centers): Bearing the economic cost; bond market in distress

## INDIA: TRIPLE SHOCK (HIGHEST ACUTE VULNERABILITY)

India's April 12 situation involved a simultaneous three-vector shock:
1. **Russian crude waiver expiry (April 11):** US 30-day waiver covering Indian purchases of Russian crude expired April 11 — one day before Islamabad collapse. India had been buying ~1.5 mb/d Russian crude (~40% of imports)
2. **Islamabad collapse (April 12):** Eliminated the diplomatic track India was passively relying on
3. **US blockade announcement (April 12–13):** Potential further Hormuz complication

**Rupee data (Zerodha, Business Standard, MUFG — high confidence):**
- Pre-war: ₹90.67/USD
- All-time low (March 30): **₹95.20/USD**
- Ceasefire recovery (April 8): ₹92.60
- Post-Islamabad (April 12): ₹93.38 (−0.7%)
- RBI capped bank net open positions at **$100M** (from up to $500M–$1B); banned new NDF contracts April 10

- 9.37 million Indians working in GCC countries; ~$42B/year in Gulf remittances (38-40% of India's total)

- UNDP (April 14): West Asia conflict threatens to push **2.5 million Indians into poverty**

## CIPS / PETROYUAN DIMENSION

**CIPS record (Disruption Banking, April 14 — financial infrastructure publication, higher confidence on transaction data):**
- Single-day CIPS record (March 2026): **1.22 trillion yuan (~$178.5B)** across 42,000 transactions
- March 2026 average daily: 920.45B yuan — **up 50% from February's 619.74B yuan**
- CIPS participants: 193 direct + 1,573 indirect across 190 countries

**But structural constraint persists:** CIPS relies on SWIFT messaging for >80% of transactions. Yuan = 3% of SWIFT payment currency share vs. 48% USD. Yuan non-convertibility (Beijing capital controls) limits true internationalization.

**mBridge (Saudi Arabia and UAE are full members):**
- Total cumulative transactions: >$55B across 4,000+ transactions
- Saudi joined 2024; UAE participating
- China's digital yuan: ~95% of settlement volume on platform
- No oil-specific mBridge data for April 2026 found

**Hormuz toll system (Atlantic Council, Bloomberg — high confidence on mechanics):**
- Fee: Up to $2M per vessel per transit
- Payment: CNY via Kunlun Bank/CIPS (outside SWIFT) OR stablecoins/Bitcoin
- Revenue: Up to **$600–800M/month** at theoretical capacity
- Still "handfuls of tankers" not expanded volume — consistent with "stress test, not scaling" framing from April 12 synthesis

The volume of yuan displacement from petrodollar flows remains modest relative to overall Hormuz volumes (which are 91% reduced from normal anyway). The regime is significant symbolically and as a precedent, not yet in scale.

## THE CRITICAL DATA GAP: APRIL 30

**The single most important missing data point:** US Treasury TIC data for February and March 2026.

- This data releases **April 30, 2026**
- Only confirmed data: Saudi Arabia reduced US Treasury holdings by **$14.7B in January 2026** (to $134.8B from $149.5B) — pre-war
- The BofA "$300B potential Gulf Treasury selling" scenario is a projection, not confirmed flows
- Saudi Arabia's decision to raise **$7B in a dollar bond deal on April 15** is the strongest behavioral counter-indicator to a Treasury-selling panic — they are *issuing* dollar-denominated debt, not fleeing dollars

**What April 30 TIC data will show:**
- Whether the war triggered accelerated Gulf Treasury liquidation (validating BofA scenario)
- Whether proceeds accumulated in dollar cash (consistent with dollar strength despite selling)
- Whether conversion to non-dollar assets has begun

Until April 30, the SWF/Treasury narrative is a scenario analysis, not an empirical finding.

## IMF SPRING MEETINGS (April 14) — INSTITUTIONAL POSITION

IEA + IMF + World Bank issued a **joint statement on April 13** — the day of the blockade announcement. This is rare institutional coordination.

- IMF global growth: Cut to **3.1%** (from 3.3% January)
- Inflation: Rising to **4.4%**
- Middle East & Central Asia: Cut by 2.8 points to **1.1%** growth
- Saudi Arabia: Cut 1.4 points to **3.1%**

**No rating agency downgrades** on any Gulf sovereign as of April 15. S&P, Moody's, Fitch all maintaining stable outlooks on UAE (Aa2), Saudi Arabia (Aa3/A+/A), Qatar (Aa2/AA−). This is itself a data point — agencies are not treating the post-Islamabad situation as a credit event yet.
